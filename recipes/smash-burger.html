Smash Burgers

Discover the secrets to crafting incredibly juicy smash burgers with perfectly crispy edges. For optimal results, avoid using meat leaner than an 80/20 ratio, and ensure it's kept extremely cold. To minimize any kitchen smoke, I recommend cooking them outdoors. Due to the high heat, these burgers cook up quickly, so be prepared and have everything ready to go beforehand!

Ingredients

  • 4 hamburger buns
  • 2 tablespoons butter, softened, or as needed
  • 1 pound ground chuck beef (80% lean)
  • 4 6-inch squares parchment paper
  • salt to taste
  • 4 slices American cheese or preferred
  • burger toppings of choice

Directions

    Step 1:

  • Preheat an outdoor grill for high heat and lightly oil the grate. Set a cast iron flat-top griddle or large cast iron skillet onto the grill and preheat until smoking.

    • Step 2:

  • Spread butter on the inside of the buns and toast on the flat-top until lightly browned. Set aside.

    • Step 3:

  • Form meat into 8 loosely-packed balls, 2 ounces each. Do not pack the meat tightly, as this will prevent it from smashing properly. Place each ball on the hot flat-top, cover with a piece of parchment (to prevent sticking to the spatula; re-use each parchment square on a second patty) nd immediately smash down to a 1/4 inch thickness using 2 stiff, sturdy spatulas that are criss-crossed to get proper leverage as you press down. You may also use the bottom of small skillet. Sprinkle the meat with salt.

    • Step 4:

  • Grill for about 45 seconds, until the edges are dark brown and the centers are a light pink color. Using a bench scraper or firm spatula, gently scrape up the patties, flip over and immediately cover 4 of them with cheese. Grill an additional 15 to 20 seconds; stack the plain patties over the cheese-covered patties so you have 4 stacks. Move each stack to a bun and serve with your favorite toppings.

Tips

  • If you only have one spatula, wrap your other hand in an old dish towel for protection, and use it to press down on the spatula with both hands.
  • You can make 4 (4 ounce) patties instead of 8 (2 ounce patties), but I prefer the thinner patties that have more crispy surface area.
  • I buy American cheese freshly-sliced from the deli, but individually-wrapped slices will work too.
